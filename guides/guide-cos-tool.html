Overview

The Child Outcome Summary (COS) Scoring Tool streamlines the federal-required COS process for Part C providers. It guides you through rating all three outcome areas, captures the supporting rationale, uses AI to help draft narrative language, and generates a clean PDF ready for the IFSP team.

🧮
Guided 7-Point Scoring
Decision trees and anchor descriptions guide you to the correct rating for each outcome area — reduces rating variability across the team.
🤖
AI Narrative Drafts
After scoring, AI generates a supporting narrative for each outcome area based on the evidence you entered. Review and edit before finalizing.
📈
Progress Over Time
Track COS ratings across multiple evaluation points to document child progress in the federal reporting format.
📄
IFSP-Ready Export
Export a formatted PDF that includes all three outcome ratings, narrative summaries, and the progress indicator for use in IFSP meetings.
🔑
Accessing the Tool

The COS Scoring Tool is accessed through the Provider Hub or directly at cos.childhoodinterventions.com. Your Hub login email is used for authentication — no separate sign-in needed when coming from the Hub.

💡
Coming from the Provider Hub: click COS Scoring Tool on your dashboard and you'll be taken in directly with your email pre-validated. Your child roster from EI Session Notes is also accessible in the COS tool.
🎚️
The 7-Point Rating Scale

COS ratings use a 7-point scale defined by federal Early Intervention guidelines. The tool includes anchor descriptions and decision guidance to help teams select the most accurate rating.

1
Child does not yet show skills in this area
2
Child shows a few early skills but they are not yet immediate foundational skills
3
Child shows some immediate foundational skills but not all — skills not age-appropriate
4
Child shows some age-appropriate skills but not consistently or across settings
5
Child's skills are age-appropriate and consistent across settings and situations
6
Child shows age-appropriate skills and some skills beyond age expectations
7
Child's skills are well beyond age expectations across all settings and situations
💡
Rating anchors: The tool shows the full anchor text and examples for each rating point. Use the Decision Guide to walk through the "would" questions that determine whether a child has age-appropriate skills in each domain.
📊
Progress indicators: For exit COS, the tool calculates the progress indicator (a, b, c) by comparing entry and exit ratings. This is automatically computed when both ratings are entered.
✅
Completing a COS
1
Select or add the child
Choose the child from your roster or add a new child record. Enter the child's initials (or first name only) and date of birth. The COS type (entry or exit) is selected here.
2
Enter evidence for Outcome A
For the Social-Emotional outcome area, describe the child's current skills in natural environments. Note both strengths and areas of concern. More specific evidence produces better AI narratives.
3
Select a rating for Outcome A
Choose a rating from 1–7. Use the anchor text and decision guide shown on screen. If your team disagrees, the tool lets you note the rating discussion.
4
Repeat for Outcomes B and C
Complete the same process for Knowledge & Skills (B) and Taking Action to Meet Needs (C). The tool steps you through each in sequence.
5
Generate narratives and export
After all three outcomes are scored, click Generate Narratives to get AI-drafted summaries. Review, edit, and then export the complete COS as a PDF.
⚠️
Team consensus required: Florida Early Steps requires COS ratings to reflect team consensus. The tool supports documentation of the discussion, but the rating entered should reflect the full IFSP team's agreed-upon score — not one person's opinion.
📝
Entry COS example — Outcome A (Social-Emotional)
Child: 18-month-old, referred for speech delay

Evidence entered: "Child makes eye contact and smiles when greeted. Reaches up to be held and calms when comforted by familiar caregiver. Does not yet point to request items or show shared attention with objects. Parallel play observed — does not initiate joint play with peers."

Rating selected: 3 — Child shows some foundational skills (emotional reciprocity, attachment) but lacks key age-expected skills (joint attention, intentional communication, peer engagement).

AI-generated narrative: "At entry, [child] demonstrates emerging social-emotional skills consistent with a rating of 3. Strengths include appropriate emotional reciprocity and caregiver attachment. Joint attention and intentional social communication are not yet present at age-expected levels and represent priority areas for early intervention."
🏁
Exit COS

An exit COS is completed when a child transitions out of Early Steps — either at age 3 or when services are discontinued. The exit COS is compared to the entry COS to calculate the federal progress indicator (a, b, or c) for each outcome area.

💡
Progress indicator is automatic: When you select "Exit" as the COS type, the tool will load the child's entry ratings and calculate the progress indicator once exit ratings are entered. No manual calculation needed.
1
Select the child and choose "Exit" as the COS type
The tool loads the child's entry ratings for reference. You'll see entry scores displayed alongside the rating scale as you score each outcome area.
2
Drop in session notes or progress summaries
Use the drop zone below to drag and drop session notes, progress summaries, or evaluation reports. The AI reads these documents to help generate evidence-based exit narratives — saving you from rewriting what's already documented.
📂
Drop session notes or progress summaries here
PDF or text files · Multiple files accepted · AI reads all uploaded content
3
Score each outcome area at exit
Rate each of the three outcomes using the 7-point scale. The entry rating is shown for comparison. Add any evidence not captured in the uploaded notes.
4
Generate exit narratives and progress indicators
Click Generate Narratives. The AI uses your uploaded notes, entered evidence, entry ratings, and exit ratings to produce a narrative that describes growth since enrollment. Progress indicators (a, b, c) are calculated and included in the export.
📊
Progress indicator key:
a — Child did not show or showed less growth than same-age peers
b — Child showed growth but did not reach age expectations
c — Child reached or surpassed age-expected skills
🤖
AI-Generated Narratives

Once ratings are complete, the tool uses the evidence you entered to draft supporting narrative language for each outcome area. These narratives explain the rating and can be included in IFSP documentation.

⚠️
Always review AI-generated narratives. The AI drafts language based on your evidence entries — but you must verify that the narrative accurately reflects the child's skills, your team's discussion, and the rating chosen. Edit anything that doesn't match.
✏️
Fully Editable
Click into any narrative to edit. Your changes are preserved as you move between sections.
🔄
Regenerate Anytime
Not happy with the draft? Click Regenerate on any outcome area to get an alternative version. Prior versions are not saved — copy before regenerating if you want to keep a draft.
📈
Tracking Progress Over Time

For children who have a prior COS on file, the tool can display entry ratings alongside current ratings to document progress. The federal progress indicator is calculated automatically.

🔐
HIPAA & Privacy
🛡️
COS data is Protected Health Information (PHI)
COS ratings linked to a specific child constitute PHI under HIPAA. All data is stored in encrypted cloud storage keyed to your login. Access is controlled by your Provider Hub account credentials. Childhood Interventions operates under HIPAA as your Business Associate.
🔑
Account-Locked Storage
COS data is stored under your account only — no other provider or organization can access it.
🤖
AI & PHI Handling
Evidence descriptions are sent to the AI for narrative generation. Use functional descriptions rather than full child names or identifying details in evidence fields.
🌐
Encrypted Connections
All data between your browser and our servers is transmitted over encrypted HTTPS connections.

Best practices:

  • Use the child's initials rather than full name in evidence fields when possible.
  • Lock your device when stepping away during a COS session.
  • Export PDFs only to HIPAA-compliant storage (organizational secure drive, not personal cloud).
  • Notify privacy@childhoodinterventions.com of any suspected unauthorized access.
