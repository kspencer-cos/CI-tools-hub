Overview

The FL-EPIC Session Guide is a free, browser-based quick reference for Florida Early Steps providers using the EPIC (Early Childhood Intervention) home visiting framework. It consolidates the core practice indicators, coaching model, FOMPA developmental targets, and Supported Engagement strategies into one tool you can pull up during or before a visit.

Unlike the other tools in the Provider Suite, this guide contains no patient data and requires no login. It's a clinical reference — think of it as a pocket guide to Florida's EI practice standards that you can access from any device, anywhere.

🆓
Free and open access. FL-EPIC is publicly available at flepic.childhoodinterventions.com. No account is required. Share it with new providers on your team, colleagues, or anyone in the Florida EI community.

Accessing the Guide

The FL-EPIC Session Guide is accessible two ways:

Direct URL
Go to flepic.childhoodinterventions.com in any browser. Works on phone, tablet, or desktop — no install needed.
🏠
Provider Hub Dashboard
Click FL-EPIC Session Guide from the Resources section of the Provider Hub — opens in a new tab with no extra login step.

The 5Q Model

The 5Q model structures the coaching conversation between provider and caregiver during each home visit. The guide provides the question prompts and reflection starters for each step.

Q1
What is the child working on?
Q2
What did the caregiver try?
Q3
What happened?
Q4
What would you change?
Q5
What will you try next?

Each question in the guide includes example prompts and follow-up questions, along with notes on how to use the cycle at different stages of a visit — opening, during activity, and close.

💡
Use the guide during session planning: Review the 5Q prompts before a visit to prepare specific questions tailored to what the family was working on since the last visit. Having targeted questions ready makes the coaching conversation feel natural rather than scripted.
🎯
FOMPA Targets

FOMPA (Florida Outcome Measurement and Practice Accountability) targets define the expected competencies and behaviors for EI providers in Florida. The guide organizes these by domain for easy lookup during session planning or professional development reflection.

🧠
By Domain
Targets are grouped by developmental and practice domain — find what you need without reading through the entire FOMPA document.
📊
Quick Reference Format
Presented as concise bullets rather than lengthy policy text — useful for coaching conversations, self-assessment, and supervision.
🤝
Supported Engagement

The Supported Engagement (SE) section covers the strategies and prompting hierarchies for supporting children's active engagement in natural environments and daily routines.

The guide covers when and how to move through the prompting hierarchy during a session, and how to coach caregivers to apply SE strategies independently between visits.

Privacy
🛡️
No PHI involved. The FL-EPIC Session Guide is a clinical reference tool only. It contains no patient data, collects no user information, and does not require a login. There are no HIPAA considerations when using or sharing this guide — it is purely educational content.
💡
Tips for Use
📅
Pre-visit prep
Open the guide 5 minutes before a visit to review the 5Q prompts and refresh on the specific FOMPA targets relevant to the child's goals for the day.
🎓
New provider onboarding
Share with new Early Steps providers as a companion to formal EPIC training. The guide condenses what can otherwise feel like overwhelming material.
🔄
Pair with Session Notes
After reviewing the SE prompting hierarchy in this guide, use EI Session Notes to document which levels were used in today's session and how the child responded.
📚
Supervision reference
Supervisors and service coordinators can use the practice indicators section as a discussion framework during reflective supervision meetings.
